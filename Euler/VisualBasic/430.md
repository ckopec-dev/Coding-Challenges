# Euler Problem 430 Solution in Visual Basic

I'll solve Euler Problem 430, which involves finding the sum of the squares of the roots of a polynomial.

## Problem Statement
Euler Problem 430 asks us to find the sum of the squares of the roots of the polynomial:
$$P(x) = x^{1000} + x^{999} + x^{998} + \ldots + x^2 + x + 1$$

## Solution Approach

To find the sum of squares of the roots, I'll use the relationship between elementary symmetric polynomials and power sums. For a monic polynomial, if we know the coefficients, we can use Newton's identities.

Let's break this down:
1. The polynomial is $P(x) = \sum_{i=0}^{1000} x^i$
2. The roots are the 1000th roots of unity (excluding 1)
3. We need to find $\sum_{i=1}^{1000} r_i^2$ where $r_i$ are the roots

## Mathematical Explanation

The solution relies on the properties of roots of unity:

1. The polynomial $P(x) = x^{1000} + x^{999} + \ldots + x + 1$ can be written as $\frac{x^{1001} - 1}{x - 1}$ for $x \neq 1$.

2. The roots of this polynomial are the 1001st roots of unity, excluding 1.

3. The sum of the squares of all 1001st roots of unity (including 1) equals 1001.

4. Since we exclude the root 1, we subtract $1^2 = 1$ from the total, giving us $1001 - 1 = 1000$.

## Final Answer

The sum of the squares of the roots of the polynomial $x^{1000} + x^{999} + \ldots + x + 1$ is **1000**.
